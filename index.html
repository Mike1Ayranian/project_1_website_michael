Daft Punk

Fun Facts! "Musique"

A New Era of Music

Daft Punk is a French electronic music duo that consists of artists Guy-Manuel de Homem-Christo and Thomas Bangalter. The French duo has been composing and performing their music since 1987. Their careers boomed in the late 90's when French electronic music and house music had achieved a great height of popularity. Releasing their first album in 1993, called "Homework," included hit "Da Funk." Their live album, "Alive 2007," from their concert at Coachella in 2007, had won a Grammy Award for Best Electronic/Dance Album. They also commposed the soundtrack for the movie "Tron: Legacy." Throughout their careers, they have composed music that has generated great popularity throughout the entire world.

"One More Time"

Daft Punk's most recent album, "Random Access Memories" had more instrumental music and included less electronically produced music than most of their songs from before. This album had also included collaborations with several artists and had hit songs such as "Get Lucky" which was collaborated with Pharrell Williams, and "Instant Crush" which was collaborated with Julian Casablancas. The album "Random Access Memories" was released in 2011, and Daft Punk has not made any music or has not released any music or news about whether or not they will be continuing to make music or stop. Their most popular song, "One More Time" has been praised by fans of the duo, as many of them are wanting Daft Punk to perform another concert yet again. Their first performance was in 1997, and their second was in 2007. Many controversies has been made about Daft Punk performing again in 2017, but they have not said anything about it yet.
